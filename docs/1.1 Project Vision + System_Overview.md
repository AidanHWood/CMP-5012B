Improving personal health is a priority for many individuals, yet maintaining consistent awareness of daily habits such as exercise, diet and weight remains difficult. Health information is often fragmented across multiple apps, some of which require high subscription fees, or is recorded inconsistently. As a result, users struggle to understand trends, set realistic goals, or maintain long-term discipline. This fragmentation can lead to reduced engagement, unrealistic expectations and unsustainable behaviour change.
The proposed Health Tracker system aims to provide a simple and integrated digital platform that enables users to record key health information, set achievable goals and monitor their progress over time without subscription barriers. The system is designed primarily for individuals who want greater clarity and motivation in managing their lifestyle habits, while remaining accessible and straightforward to use.
In addition to supporting individual users, the system also considers wider stakeholder interests. The commissioning insurance organisation intends to explore this application as a business experiment, with potential future integration into preventative health services or incentive-based schemes. Other stakeholders include healthcare professionals, system maintainers and regulatory bodies who are concerned with data accuracy, security and ethical handling of personal health information.
The value of the Health Tracker lies in providing a unified and accessible way to capture health data, generate meaningful feedback and support users in understanding their progress over time. By focusing on simplicity, clarity and motivational feedback, the system aims to encourage sustainable behaviour change while also supporting future organisational and business objectives.
