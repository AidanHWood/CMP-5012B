## 1.4 Feature Matrix of Similar Systems

| Feature | MyFitnessPal | Strava | Fitbit | Couch to 5K | Apple Health | Health Tracker (Proposed) |
|----------|--------------|--------|--------|--------------|--------------|----------------------------|
| User account / login | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Record exercise sessions | Limited | Yes | Yes | Guided only | Yes | Yes |
| Record diet / calories | Yes (Strong) | No | Limited | No | Limited (Manual input) | Yes |
| Weight tracking | Yes | No | Yes | No | Yes | Yes |
| Goal setting | Yes | Yes | Yes | Programme-based | Limited | Yes |
| Goal deadline tracking | Limited | No | Limited | Yes | No | Yes |
| Historical trend graphs | Yes | Yes | Yes | Limited | Yes (Strong visualisation) | Yes |
| Group / social features | Limited | Strong | Moderate | No | Minimal | Yes (Opt-in groups) |
| Email notifications | Limited | Yes | Yes | No | Limited | Yes |
| Wearable device dependency | No | No | Yes (Device-based) | No | Partial (Apple devices integrate best) | No |
| Subscription required for full features | Yes (Premium) | Yes (Premium) | Yes (Device + Premium) | No | No | No |

The comparison shows that most existing platforms focus heavily on one specific area of health tracking rather than providing a balanced solution. MyFitnessPal emphasises calorie tracking and premium features, while Strava focuses on performance and competitive social elements. Fitbit offers broader monitoring but relies on proprietary devices. Apple Health effectively consolidates data and provides strong visualisation, however it primarily displays information rather than actively supporting behaviour change. Couch to 5K delivers structured guidance but is limited to running programmes.
This highlights a gap for a simplified system that combines exercise, diet, weight tracking and structured goal monitoring in one place without hardware dependency or subscription barriers. The proposed Health Tracker addresses this by integrating core tracking features with clear goal management and optional group functionality, while remaining accessible and easy to use.
